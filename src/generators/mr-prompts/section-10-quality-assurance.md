# Section 10 — Quality Assurance: Specific Instructions

You are generating **Section 10 (Quality Assurance)** of an Agricultural Census Metadata Review.

## Keywords for evidence retrieval

quality, pilot, pre-test, training, supervisor, supervision, monitoring, back-check, re-interview, verification, field check, review, coverage check, non-response, response rate

## What to cover

Describe the quality assurance mechanisms implemented throughout the census operation. Address all of the following where documented:

1. **Pilot testing** — describe any pilot test or pre-test of the questionnaire or data collection procedures: when it was conducted, geographic scope, and what changes resulted.

2. **Training of field staff** — describe the training provided to enumerators and supervisors: duration, content, who delivered it, and how many staff were trained (where documented).

3. **Supervision and monitoring** — describe the supervision arrangements during fieldwork (e.g. one supervisor per N enumerators, daily check-ins, GPS tracking of field visits, dashboard monitoring of submission rates).

4. **Re-interviews or back-checks** — describe any systematic re-interview or back-check of a sample of completed interviews to verify data quality: the proportion of interviews checked and the procedure used.

5. **Computerised management or monitoring systems** — describe any digital systems used for real-time fieldwork monitoring (e.g. submission tracking, coverage dashboards, automated alerts).

## Writing rules for this section

- Describe quality mechanisms in the order they occur in the census cycle (design → pilot → training → fieldwork → post-collection).
- Include documented numbers (e.g. percentage of forms back-checked, ratio of supervisors to enumerators) only where explicitly stated.
- Do not describe data editing or imputation — those belong in Section 9.
- If a mechanism is not documented, write exactly: *"Information on this point was not available in the source documents provided."*
- Write in formal, neutral, third-person prose.
