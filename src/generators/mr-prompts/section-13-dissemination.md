# Section 13 — Dissemination of Census Results and Microdata: Specific Instructions

You are generating **Section 13 (Dissemination of Census Results and Microdata)** of an Agricultural Census Metadata Review.

## Keywords for evidence retrieval

publication, dissemination, release, report, preliminary, final, statistical table, microdata, anonymised, access, website, open data, portal, volume, bulletin, press release, date

## What to cover

Describe all aspects of how census results were disseminated. Address all of the following where documented:

### Publications

1. **Preliminary results** — describe any preliminary or advance release: title, date of release, format (print, PDF, online).
2. **Final publications** — list all final census publications: full title, date of publication, format, and publisher. If multiple volumes or thematic reports were published, list each.
3. **Statistical tables** — describe the availability and format of statistical tables (e.g. published in the main report, available as separate Excel/CSV files, accessible via an online database).

### Microdata

4. **Microdata availability** — state whether anonymised microdata are publicly available or available on request.
5. **Access conditions** — describe the conditions under which microdata may be accessed (e.g. open download, registered-user access, on-site use only, not available).
6. **Microdata format** — state the format(s) in which microdata are available (e.g. SPSS, Stata, CSV).

### Dissemination platform

7. **Official website or platform** — provide the URL of the official dissemination platform where documented. If multiple platforms are used, list each with its purpose.

## Writing rules for this section

- State publication dates in full (e.g. *December 2023*) where documented.
- Include URLs only when explicitly stated in the source documents.
- Do not describe data archiving arrangements — those belong in Section 11.
- If dissemination information is not documented, write exactly: *"Information on this point was not available in the source documents provided."*
- Write in formal, neutral, third-person prose.
